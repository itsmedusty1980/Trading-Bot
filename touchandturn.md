Here is the breakdown of the Touch and Turn Scalper, a mechanical strategy designed to take advantage of the first 90 minutes of the market open.
Step 1: "Fibonaccify" the Opening Range

Open the asset you want to trade on a 15-minute chart.
Let the first 15 minutes of the market open pass so the opening candlestick completely closes.
Draw a Fibonacci retracement from the absolute highest price point (top of the wick) to the absolute lowest price point (bottom of the wick) of that single 15-minute candlestick, and extend the lines into the future.
Pay special attention to the 38.2 and 61.8 Fibonacci levels, as these will serve as your target profit levels later.
Step 2: Confirm the "Liquidity Candle"

This step ensures the opening move was a "manipulation candle," an aggressive price push engineered by institutions to hit retail stop-losses and generate the liquidity they need to enter large positions.
Switch to a daily chart and apply the Average True Range (ATR) indicator using the default 14-day setting to find the asset's average daily movement.
Calculate 25% of that daily ATR.
Check the total size (high to low) of your 15-minute opening candlestick. If the size of the candle is equal to or greater than 25% of the daily ATR, it is confirmed as a valid liquidity candle.
Step 3: Dial in the Perfect Trade

Drop down to a 1-minute chart for precise entry control.
You will place a limit order at the edge of the opening range, aiming to trade in the opposite direction of the opening manipulation candle.
For Longs: If the 15-minute opening candle was red (negative), place a long limit order at the absolute low of the range.
For Shorts: If the 15-minute opening candle was green (positive), place a short limit order at the absolute high of the range.
Set Your Take Profit: Place your target profit at the 38.2 Fibonacci level (for longs) or the 61.8 Fibonacci level (for shorts).
Set Your Stop Loss: Configure your stop loss to ensure a 2:1 reward-to-risk ratio. This means the distance to your take profit should be exactly twice the size of your stop loss.
Ensure your entry happens strictly within the first 90 minutes of the market open
